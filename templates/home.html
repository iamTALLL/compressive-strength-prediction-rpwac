INTRODUCTION

In the 21st century, humanity faces the plastic waste crisis and the need for sustainable construction materials. Plastic waste breaks down due to physical forces, causing serious environmental problems. Governments and ministries have issued policies to reduce the impact of plastic waste on the environment.

Recycled plastic waste aggregate concrete is a promising solution to replace traditional materials, reducing plastic waste while conserving natural resources. Plastic is used in concrete in two ways: as aggregate and fibers, providing economic benefits and environmental friendliness.

RESEARCH OBJECTIVES

This research aims to predict the compressive strength of concrete using recycled plastic by:

  • Using various machine learning models
  • Integrating optimization algorithms to improve accuracy
  • Developing a user-friendly web application

Research object: Concrete using recycled plastic aggregate
Research scope: Compressive strength of concrete

ABOUT THE PREDICTION MODEL

The study is based on a dataset of 257 laboratory compression tests. After evaluating multiple machine learning models, XGBoost demonstrated superior performance.

Initial results: R² = 0.921, RMSE = 3.648 MPa, MAE = 2.918 MPa.
After hyperparameter optimization using Whale Optimization Algorithm:
R² = 0.94, RMSE ≈ 3.18 MPa, MAE ≈ 2.41 MPa.

These results confirm the effectiveness of combining metaheuristic algorithms in predicting the compressive strength of recycled concrete.

KEY FEATURES

Sustainable and environmentally friendly

Reuse plastic waste, reduce pressure on natural resources, and promote sustainable development in construction.

Optimized artificial intelligence

Apply XGBoost combined with WOA, achieving high accuracy (R² = 0.94).

High accuracy and reliability

Low error rates, meeting the requirements for analysis and real-world applications.
